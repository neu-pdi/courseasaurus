## Why Semester-Long Projects Matter

<div style={{textAlign: 'left', padding: '0 60px', fontSize: '0.9em'}}>

## Stage 1: Generate Project Concepts

<div style={{textAlign: 'left', fontSize: '0.85em', padding: '0 40px'}}>

### Working in a Course Repository

**Key advantage:** AI reads your actual course files

```markdown
Prompt: "Examine course structure. Generate 3-4 
semester-long project concepts that integrate these 
learning objectives throughout the term. Keep brief: 
2-3 paragraphs per concept."
```

<div style={{marginTop: '30px', padding: '15px', backgroundColor: '#2ecc71', color: 'white', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>Your job:</strong> Select the most pedagogically sound concept
  <ul style={{marginTop: '10px', marginBottom: '0'}}>
    <li>Student engagement</li>
    <li>Real-world relevance</li>
    <li>Portfolio value</li>
    <li>Skill integration</li>
    <li>Feasibility</li>

## Stage 2: Identify Natural Milestones

<div style={{textAlign: 'left', fontSize: '0.8em', padding: '0 40px'}}>

```markdown
Prompt: "I've chosen the [concept] project. Examine 
course structure. Identify 4-5 natural milestone points. 
For each:
- Suggested due week (AFTER required skills taught)
- Available skills from lectures
- High-level deliverable (one sentence)
- Why this is a natural breaking point
- Time estimate (3-6 hours)

Save to assignments/project-overview.md"
```

<div style={{marginTop: '25px', padding: '15px', backgroundColor: '#e74c3c', color: 'white', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>‚ö†Ô∏è Critical Review Checkpoint</strong>
  <ul style={{marginTop: '10px', marginBottom: '0', fontSize: '0.95em'}}>
    <li>Verify timing relative to exams/breaks</li>
    <li>Check milestone spacing</li>
    <li>Ensure progressive difficulty</li>
    <li><strong>Manually edit for your voice and expectations</strong></li>
  </ul>
</div>

<p style={{marginTop: '15px', fontSize: '0.85em'}}>
  <code>git commit -m "Add project overview with milestone timeline"</code>

## Stage 3: Expand Each Milestone

<div style={{textAlign: 'left', fontSize: '0.75em', padding: '0 40px'}}>

**Work on ONE milestone at a time, not all at once**

```markdown
Prompt: "@project-overview.md has our overview. 
Create detailed requirements at 
@project-milestone-1.md for Milestone 1 only.

Include:
- Skills learned by Week 3 (reference lectures)
- Specific deliverables (files, docs, features)
- Acceptance criteria ("done" definition)
- Time estimate (3-5 hours)
- Why this milestone matters pedagogically"
```

### Edit for Specificity

<div style={{display: 'grid', gridTemplateColumns: '1fr 1fr', gap: '15px', marginTop: '15px'}}>
  <div style={{padding: '12px', backgroundColor: '#ffebee', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
    <strong>‚ùå AI draft:</strong><br/>
    "Create appropriate documentation"
  </div>

    <strong>‚úÖ You edit:</strong><br/>
    "Create README.md with: title, description (2-3 sentences), 5+ features, tech stack. Use markdown from Lecture 2."
  </div>
</div>

## Stage 4: Lecture-Grounded Rubrics

<div style={{textAlign: 'left', fontSize: '0.8em', padding: '0 40px'}}>

```markdown
Prompt: "Create grading rubric for 
@project-milestone-3.md (20 points) that:

- Assesses technical correctness
- Focuses on NEW material since Milestone 2
- Uses code standards from 
  @lecture-notes/l2-best-practices.md
- References specific lectures for each criterion"
```

<div style={{marginTop: '30px', padding: '20px', backgroundColor: '#f39c12', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>Why Reference Lectures Explicitly?</strong>
  <ul style={{marginTop: '10px', marginBottom: '0'}}>
    <li>Uses same terminology students learned</li>
    <li>Criteria grounded in what was taught</li>
    <li>Students can review specific lectures</li>
    <li>Makes grading feel predictable, not arbitrary</li>
    <li>TAs grade consistently</li>
  </ul>
</div>

</div>

## Stage 5: Generate Support Materials

<div style={{textAlign: 'left', fontSize: '0.85em', padding: '0 40px'}}>

```markdown
Prompt: "Generate support materials for 
@project-milestone-2.md:

- 'Getting Started' ‚Äî concrete first steps
- 'Common Pitfalls' ‚Äî typical errors and fixes
- 'Resources' ‚Äî links to docs, tutorials, examples

Save to project-milestone-2-support.md"
```

### Additional Support AI Can Generate

- **FAQ** (10 common questions + answers)
- **Troubleshooting Guide** (common errors + fixes)
- **Simplified Example** (different domain, demonstrates concept)
- **Rubric Explanation** (how to earn full credit)

## Common Pitfalls

<div style={{fontSize: '0.75em', textAlign: 'left', padding: '0 40px'}}>

<h3>‚ùå Watch Out For:</h3>

<div className="fragment" style={{marginBottom: '15px', padding: '12px', backgroundColor: '#ffebee', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>1. Front-Loaded Difficulty</strong> ‚Äî First milestone too ambitious<br/>
  <em style={{fontSize: '0.9em'}}>‚Üí Ask AI: "Is milestone-1 achievable after lectures 1-3?"</em>
</div>

<div className="fragment" style={{marginBottom: '15px', padding: '12px', backgroundColor: '#fff3e0', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>2. Disconnected Milestones</strong> ‚Äî Students start over each time<br/>
  <em style={{fontSize: '0.9em'}}>‚Üí Ask AI: "Verify each milestone builds on previous deliverables"</em>
</div>

<div className="fragment" style={{marginBottom: '15px', padding: '12px', backgroundColor: '#f3e5f5', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>3. Skills Gap</strong> ‚Äî Milestone requires skills not yet taught<br/>
  <em style={{fontSize: '0.9em'}}>‚Üí Ask AI: "Verify all required skills taught before Week 12"</em>
</div>

<div className="fragment" style={{marginBottom: '15px', padding: '12px', backgroundColor: '#e3f2fd', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>4. No Feedback Loop</strong> ‚Äî Grades without actionable guidance<br/>
  <em style={{fontSize: '0.9em'}}>‚Üí Grade within 1 week; use AI feedback templates</em>
</div>

<div className="fragment" style={{marginBottom: '15px', padding: '12px', backgroundColor: '#e8f5e9', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>5. Unrealistic Time Estimates</strong> ‚Äî "Should take 3 hours" (takes 10)<br/>
  <em style={{fontSize: '0.9em'}}>‚Üí Test-solve yourself. Add 50-100% buffer.</em>
</div>

<div className="fragment" style={{padding: '12px', backgroundColor: '#fce4ec', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  <strong>6. Vague Requirements</strong> ‚Äî Students don't know when "done"<br/>
  <em style={{fontSize: '0.9em'}}>‚Üí Ask AI: "Identify vague requirements; suggest measurable alternatives"</em>
</div>

</div>

## Key Takeaways

<div style={{textAlign: 'left', fontSize: '0.9em', padding: '0 60px', marginTop: '30px'}}>

1. **Staged Artifact Development** ‚Äî Build incrementally with review checkpoints
2. **Learning-Aligned Milestones** ‚Äî Each uses skills just taught
3. **Lecture-Grounded Rubrics** ‚Äî Reference specific lectures for predictability
4. **Human Judgment is Essential** ‚Äî AI drafts, you refine and decide
5. **Test-Solve Yourself** ‚Äî If you can't do it in allocated time, neither can students
6. **Be Responsive** ‚Äî Adapt mid-semester based on student progress

<div style={{marginTop: '30px', padding: '20px', backgroundColor: 'rgba(255,255,255,0.2)', borderRadius: '8px', fontSize: '0.95em'}}>
  <strong>Remember:</strong> AI handles drafting and structure.<br/>
  You provide pedagogy, refinement, and final judgment.

## The Meta Lesson: AI Philosophy

<div style={{textAlign: 'left', fontSize: '0.85em', padding: '0 60px'}}>

### What This Process Models

<div style={{marginTop: '25px', padding: '20px', backgroundColor: '#ecf0f1', borderRadius: '8px'}}>
  
**‚úÖ Effective AI Usage:**
- Only ask for what you can review for accuracy
- Break large tasks into reviewable chunks
- Iterate on scope when results unsatisfactory
- Maintain expertise through direct practice
- Make conscious decisions about assistance boundaries

**‚ùå Ineffective AI Usage:**
- "Generate complete project" ‚Üí unreviewed bulk content
- No editorial checkpoints
- Loss of pedagogical voice
- Skill atrophy from over-delegation
